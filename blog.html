Agile Methodology for ADB Managed Services Implementation

Full methodology playbook for Agile + CAB-aligned managed services delivery.

Published by Bibin Skaria · Feb 2025

Download .docx

1. Introduction

This document outlines the Agile methodology to be followed for the Asian Development Bank (ADB) Managed Services engagement. It aligns with ADB’s SDLC standards and supports the delivery of L3 support, routine maintenance, and enhancements for Microsoft-based applications.

2. Agile Framework Overview

ADB employs an Agile-Scrum approach for iterative and incremental delivery. The Managed Service Provider (MSP) will integrate with ADB’s existing product teams and collaborate closely during the entire development lifecycle.

Key Elements:

Sprint Length: 2 weeks

Tools Used: Azure DevOps for backlog management, sprint tracking, and CI/CD

Team Structure: Product Owner (ADB), Scrum Master (MSP), Development Team (MSP)

Artifacts: Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment, Burndown Charts

3. Agile Roles & Responsibilities

Product Owner (ADB): Defines user stories, prioritizes backlog, approves increments

Scrum Master (MSP): Facilitates Agile ceremonies, removes impediments

Development Team (MSP): Delivers shippable increment each sprint

Testing Partner (ADB/Third Party): Executes SIT, UAT, performance testing

4. Agile Ceremonies

Sprint Planning: Define sprint scope, assign story points

Daily Standup: 15-minute sync on progress, blockers, and plans

Sprint Review: Demo completed work to stakeholders

Sprint Retrospective: Analyze what went well and identify improvements

5. Agile Artifacts

Artifact

Description

Product Backlog

All feature/user story requests

Sprint Backlog

Stories committed for the sprint

Burndown Chart

Tracks sprint progress

Increment

Shippable deliverables

Definition of Done

Includes unit test, peer review, successful deployment to staging

6. Agile Process Flow

Product Owner creates User Stories

Stories are added to Product Backlog in Azure DevOps

During Sprint Planning, team selects stories for the Sprint Backlog

Development, unit testing, documentation, and peer review are done

Code is committed via CI pipeline for SIT/UAT

Sprint Review and Retrospective

7. Agile Metrics & KPIs

Velocity: Measure story points completed per sprint

Sprint Burndown: Track remaining work

Defect Leakage: Track bugs in production vs. QA

Cycle Time: Time from story start to completion

8. Quality Assurance Integration

Unit Testing by MSP

SIT & UAT by ADB’s Testing Partner

Automated Testing using Azure DevOps pipelines

Continuous Integration/Delivery aligned with CAB approval workflows

9. Change Management Integration

All enhancements require RFC approval from CAB

Change plans must be aligned with sprint cycles

Risk assessments must be done and documented prior to release

10. Continuous Improvement

Lessons learned captured during Sprint Retrospective

Monthly performance reports reviewed jointly by ADB and MSP

Action items tracked for recurring issues and improvements

This Agile Methodology will serve as a living document and evolve with the engagement. It ensures responsiveness, flexibility, and transparency throughout the lifecycle of support and enhancements for ADB’s Microsoft-based solutions.

Change Management — Business-as-Usual Framework

Structured RFC intake, CAB governance, and SLA tracking for managed services operations.

Published by Bibin Skaria · Mar 2025

Download .docx

Overview:

The Change and Release Management Process ensures structured planning, approval, and execution of changes within the project lifecycle without negatively impacting business continuity.

Phases:

Change Request Initiation: Raised via RFC (Request for Change) in the Change Management System.

Impact Assessment: Includes risk, rollback, and resource availability analysis.

CAB Review: All changes are reviewed by the Change Advisory Board (CAB).

Approval & Scheduling: Changes are approved and planned during maintenance windows.

Implementation: Conducted by technical teams following DevSecOps practices.

Post-Implementation Review: Validation, documentation update, and stakeholder feedback.

Types of Changes:

Standard (pre-approved)

Emergency

Normal

Tools:

Azure DevOps / JIRA for tracking

Email notifications for CAB updates

2. Performance Reviews Deliverables & SLAs

Review Frequency:

Monthly and quarterly reviews with ADB and stakeholders

Key Deliverables:

Monthly SLA Compliance Reports

Issue/Incident Trends & RCA Summaries

DevOps KPIs (deployment frequency, MTTR, change failure rate)

Test Coverage & Quality Metrics

Capacity Planning Insights

Continuous Improvement Initiatives

Sample SLAs:

Critical Incident Response: < 15 mins

Incident Resolution: 90% within agreed timeframe

CI/CD Pipeline Uptime: 99.5%

Release Deployment Success Rate: > 98%

3. SLAs and Measurement Criteria for BaU Managed Services Work

Service Scope:

Ongoing maintenance, feature enhancement, bug fixes, system optimization

Proposed SLAs:

Uptime & Availability: 99.5%

Incident Management:

Priority 1: Response in 15 mins, resolution within 4 hours

Priority 2: Response in 30 mins, resolution within 8 hours

Priority 3: Response in 1 hour, resolution within 24 hours

Change Request Turnaround:

Minor: within 3 business days

Major: within 7–10 business days post-approval

Measurement Metrics:

SLA Compliance %

Average Time to Resolve

% of Tickets Resolved Within SLA

Change Success Rate

Downtime Incidents per Quarter

Reporting:

Dashboards shared monthly

Bi-weekly status calls

Quarterly performance reviews
